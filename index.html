Who We Are

Health Service Nepal (HSN) is a non-profit, non- governmental organization established and registered under organization registration act 2034 at District administration office, Lalitpur, Nepal and affiliated to Social Welfare Council.

HSN is a member-based organization that has been working in the area of Sexual and Reproductive Health, Non-Communicable Diseases, competent development of Human Resource for Health, and protection of professional rights of Health Care Workforce for attaining optimal health of the people of Nepal since its establishment in 2020.

HSN has brought a new and sacred concept of Medicine bank that encourages donation of medicine and other medical supplies for people who have poor access to medicines and free health services. Our unique community service and health development model apparently connects and satisfies both donor and receiver in the process of medicine donation and providing other necessary medical support.

HSN works to generate evidences, and promotes health & healthy behavior of the people through research study, media & publications, advocacy, awareness & sensitization; trainings and social mobilization in a holistic manner by and with concerned stakeholders.

Our Vision

HSN envisions a community where all individuals, especially poor, vulnerable, deprived and socially excluded, attain a healthy and productive life, and thus our theme generates as “Nobody will die in absence of medicines and health services”

Our Mission

HSN seeks to build a society especially targeting needy people by making available comprehensive development programmes on health and healthy behavior and providing Medicine Bank services.

Our Objectives

provide health-related preventive, promotive, curative and palliative services to ensure the availability of essential drugs and health services for needy people.
focus on primary health care to improve access to quality essential services.
co-ordination and collaboration between relevant organizations to facilitate and commence medicine collection and distributions.
providing various public health related programs, psychosocial counseling, suggestions and advice for general Peoples.
conducting trainings, seminars and workshops and health campaign and awareness programmes for enhancing the professional capacity of health workers and those involved in health services.
generate evidences through researches in health and development focused on Sexual and Reproductive Health.
build up /or expand the capacity and network of the organizations working in the field of Non-communicable Diseases, disasters management and accidents.
